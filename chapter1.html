Chapter 1

As she stepped onto the schoolground, every gaze fell upon her. The new girl has arrived. Mara Grager, a transfer student from Germany, coming to Australia for 9 months. Brown luscious hair, eyes sparkling like the stars on a clear night sky. Looking down at her outfit is a beautiful sundress she wears with her favourite ballerina shoes. Her chaotic bag hanging on her shoulder slides down as she looks at her new schedule. "History…", she mumbles to herself, navigating through the school to find her homeroom.

There she is, walking around school, confused and disoriented, when she suddenly bumps into a mysterious figure while rounding a corner. She was about to stumble to the ground, when a pair of hands grab her waist and saves her from the fall. Her eyes slowly open to reveal a bright smile on a stupid-looking face. "Yo alright, mate?" the Aussie almost shouts, while letting go of her. He leans against the wall to appear nonchalant, since he doesnt recognize the face. Before she could even answer, he introduces himself. "Haven't seen you around here, you new? Im Jason Smithy but you can just call me McSmith, everyone does around here heh." He sees a guy walking by and dabs him up, then turns back to her, looking way too cocky. Mara looks absolutely unimpressed and chuckles awkwardly. "Right.. Wo bin ich hier gelandet..", she mumbles. She pulls herself together and excuses herself, to get away from him.

Arrived in class, she sits down in the corner of the class beside a window, looking outside and avoiding anyones gaze. "I wonder what my friends are doing right now..", she thinks to herself, although she gets interrupted by the teacher walking in. "Attention everyone! We have a new student today, who will be accompanying us for the next 9 months! Do you want to introduce yourself?". He points to Mara. She stands up, "Hey guys, i'm Mara and come from Germany, it's nice to meet you." And she sits down. The teacher looks at her excitedly and nods "You know, I know German! Yeah my great great grandmother was German! Uhm ick lieba wurcht (Ich liebe Wurst)! Ain't that right, mate?" Mara nods hesitantly, not understanding what he just said. "Thats right..?" He starts teaching for the lesson, talking about the WW2 and occasionally trying to make a joke about Mara being German, which fails to make her even chuckle, she knew she had to endure this for a whole hour. She sighs.
